# SALA • SAFE-LOAD ANALYTICS (SALA) 🚛❄️ ### Predictive Intelligence for Norwegian Logistics **Official Portal:** [www.safelast.no](http://www.safelast.no) **Location:** Larvik, Norway **Author:** Paulo Fernando De Barros --- ## 🎯 The Mission SALA is a proprietary predictive framework designed to quantify and mitigate ergonomic risks in the Norwegian transport sector. We bridge the gap between traditional safety protocols and modern predictive data to reduce long-term sickness claims (sykemelding) and protect driver health. ## 🧠 The Intelligence (Algorithm) Unlike static safety manuals, SALA uses a dynamic algorithm to calculate the **Ergonomic Risk Index ($R_i$)** in real-time. ### Mathematical Framework: $R_i = (S_f \cdot L_w) + (F_i \cdot T_h)$ - **Sf (Surface Factor):** Dynamic coefficients for sub-arctic conditions (Snow, Slush, Mud). - **Lw (Load Weight):** Impact of total tonnage handled. - **Fi (Fatigue Index):** Cumulative metabolic cost. - **Th (Time/Hours):** Duration of continuous physical activity. --- ## 🚀 Key Strategic Benefits ### For Transport Companies * **Operational ROI:** Lower insurance premiums and reduced turnover. * **Compliance:** Full alignment with **Arbeidsmiljøloven** (The Working Environment Act). ### For Unions & Drivers (YTF/NTF) * **Career Longevity:** Data-backed evidence to prevent Musculoskeletal Disorders (MSDs). * **Safety First:** Real-time "Go/No-Go" decision support for physical loading. ### For NAV & Regulatory Bodies * **Preventive Data:** Precision analytics for public health intervention in the logistics sector. --- ## 🇳🇴 Norwegian Standards Alignment The SALA project is developed to complement the **YSK (Statens vegvesen)** professional competence standards, ensuring that data insights are practical, legal, and culturally relevant to the Norwegian workplace. ## 🛠 Tech Stack * **Analytics:** Predictive Modeling / Ergonomic Risk Scoring * **Domain:** Logistics & Occupational Health * **Documentation:** Technical Thesis & Methodology --- © 2026 SALA - Safe-Load Analytics.
