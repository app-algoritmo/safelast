Reference: BIOM-FATIGUE-01A

Technical Note: Baseline Fatigue Initialization

Subject: Rationale for the 2.0-hour default initialization in the Driver View.

1. The "Cold Start" Fallacy

In occupational biomechanics, a "zero-risk" state only exists after a period of restorative sleep. For logistics professionals, the manual handling phase (loading/unloading) never starts at 0% fatigue. The 2.0h baseline accounts for the Pre-Task Metabolic Load.

2. Whole-Body Vibration (WBV)

Driving for 1 to 2 hours to reach a delivery point in Larvik or the Nordic corridor subjects the spine to constant low-frequency vibrations. According to ISO 2631-1, these vibrations cause:

  • Micro-swelling of intervertebral discs.
  • Reduced muscular tonus in the multifidus (stabilizing muscles).

3. The Safety Factor (Sf) Multiplier

Initial_Ri = (WBV_Exposure * 0.15) + (Metabolic_Basal_Rate)

Starting the app at 2.0h ensures the Risk Index (Ri) is calculated with a conservative safety margin. This prevents the "First Lift Injury" syndrome, where a driver, feeling deceptively rested, performs a heavy lift with "cold" and vibration-fatigued tendons.

4. Managerial Oversight

Managers should interpret an initial Ri above 0% as a calibrated safety buffer. This ensures that when the driver performs high-frequency deliveries, the cumulative risk curve is accurate and not underestimated.
